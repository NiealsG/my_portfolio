The Problem

Free.Fi was created to help remote workers. Specifically, the following issues were addressed:

  • I need a free workspace: A certain portion of the remote work force may not be interested in paying for a subscription to a professional co-working office, or may require an appropriate space to work from for a couple of hours.
  • Is a coffee shop suited to working remotely from?: Not all coffee shops are appropriate for the remote worker. The noise levels may be too high, the wifi quality may be poor, or a myriad of other concerns. If a worker is new to the concept of remote working, or new to a city, they may not have yet identified suitable options.

The Solution

Free.Fi is the perfect companion for any frugal remote worker. Using the users phone’s inbuilt GPS, Free.Fi will search it’s database and suggest coffee shops that are nearby and suited to work from.

Competitive Analysis

The research process began with competitive analysis. We examined 3 websites and apps which helped remote workers find co-working spaces - OOTD, Coworker and Workfrom. Below is a summary of the

A summary of the SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities and Threats) analysis for each competitor has been included below.

Screenshot of OOTD on mobile

OOTD is an app aimed at helping remote workers discover and save non-office work spaces near them. There is also a localised chat feature to connect users.

Strengths

  • All nearby locations are listed, so even if there are no ‘discovered’ locations near the user, they can still locate a workspace.
  • Users are able to rate locations.
  • Simple design and offers a user-friendly experience.

Weaknesses

  • The symbols shown on the search function are confusing.
  • As it’s a new app, depending on a user’s location there may be a lack of ‘discovered’ locations.

Opportunities

  • Potential to grow the number of ‘discovered’ listings.
  • Potential to expand to include a website.

Threats

  • Competitor sites with more verified listings offer a more comprehensive alternative.
  • The growth of competitor sites.
Screenshot of CoWorker on mobile

Coworker’s mission is to help accelerate the adoption of coworking, through enabling discovery.

Strengths

  • Over 14 000 verified work spaces listed.
  • Easy-to-use website.
  • Because only paid-for working spaces are listed, the focus is on depth of information, not breadth. Each listing is very detailed.

Weaknesses

  • There is no option to connect with other remote workers.

Opportunities

  • Potential to include a networking feature for remote workers.
  • Grow to include listings of free work-friendly spaces.

Threats

  • The growth of competitor sites
Screenshot of Workfrom on mobile

Workfrom’s mission is to support distributed and independent workforces.

Strengths

  • Slack channel for paying members, so users can connect with other remote workers.
  • Listings include detailed information about the Wifi, something not shown on competitor sites.

Weaknesses

  • Listings can be added by any user, so quality of information and entries may vary.
  • There are icons displayed in the header on the website, but the purpose of these icons is not immediately understood.

Opportunities

  • Potential to offer bookings directly through the website/app.
  • Potential to include information about fees for paid working spaces.

Threats

  • Competitor sites with more verified listings offer a more comprehensive alternative.
  • The growth of competitor sites.

Overall Coworker offered the most comprehensive solution, except that free locations were not included. This highlights a gap in the market - there is space for a similar offering that focuses on free working spaces.

Branding

While usability testing on the wireframes was carried out, the work started on developing the brand. This encompassed the app name, colours, logo brand emotions, mood board, logo iterations and style guide.

The Name & Logo

The name “Free.Fi” was conceived. It was born from the concept of “WiFi” and “Freedom” - two elements that are extremely important to remote workers.

The Free.Fi logo mark was conceptualised by incorporating the WiFi symbol and “i” from Free.Fi. It’s simple, clear and fuss-free, just like Free.Fi.

Free.Fi aims to provide a simple, easy and fuss-free solution. To express this with the branding, a bright but minimalist approach was taken.

Usability Testing

Usability testing was carried out on the hi-fi mockup prototype. Participants were asked to perform three tasks, each a high priority user story. These were:

  • Finding a workspace near me
  • Saving a workspace to access later
  • Read the reviews of a workspace

Four tests were carried out in person. In each case, the respondents struggled with the second task. Initially the design used a bookmark icon, which was to be clicked on to save the listing. None of the respondents easily identified the bookmark icon as a method for saving. Due to this struggle, the design was changed. A ‘save’ button replaced the bookmark icon.

The changes made to the listing screen after user testing

In addition, the text “Find a free workspace” on the home screen was adjusted so it would direct the user upon clicking to the search page.

The changes made to the homepage after user testing

Conclusion

When looking at the problems we were confronted with at the beginning of the project, we can say that the final prototype solves these issues. Free.Fi offers an elegant and functional option for remote workers to locate free workspaces near them. There is also room for growth in the future - the app could be expanded to include an option to book with the coffee shops via the app. Also there could be a message board or chat room, so that remote workers could communicate directly with each other.
